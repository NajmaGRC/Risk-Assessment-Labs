## 🗂️ Project 1 — Quantitative Risk Analysis: SecureBank Financial

**File:** `SecureBank-Quantitative-Risk-Analysis.pdf`

Applied quantitative risk analysis for SecureBank Financial — calculating Annualised Loss Expectancy (ALE) across multiple vulnerabilities including an API authentication bypass with a potential single incident loss of $5,000,000.

**Techniques applied:**
- Single Loss Expectancy (SLE) calculation
- Annual Rate of Occurrence (ARO) modelling
- Annualised Loss Expectancy (ALE) — full risk exposure quantification
- Risk prioritisation and treatment recommendations

**Why this matters:** Quantitative risk analysis translates cybersecurity risk into financial language that boards and executives understand — the foundation of risk-informed decision making.

## 🗂️ Project 2 — Phishing Simulation & Risk Quantification

**File:** `Phishing-Simulation-Risk-Analysis.pdf`

Advanced GRC lab combining a phishing simulation with quantitative risk analysis. Calculated real campaign metrics across four phases and translated human behaviour data into measurable organisational risk.

**Metrics calculated:**
- Email open rate: 82% | Click-through rate: 47%
- Credential submission rate: 18%
- Full ALE modelling based on simulation outcomes
- Risk treatment recommendations mapped to findings

**Frameworks applied:** `NIST SP 800-30` `Quantitative Risk Analysis`

## 🗂️ Project 3 — Live Risk Monitoring with Wazuh (SIEM/FIM)

**File:** `Wazuh-Risk-Monitoring-Lab.pdf`

Hands-on lab deploying Wazuh — an open-source SIEM and endpoint detection platform — to operationalise risk monitoring through File Integrity Monitoring (FIM). Deployed Wazuh Manager on Ubuntu, connected a Windows agent, and monitored real-time file change events.

**What was built and demonstrated:**
- Wazuh Manager deployment on Ubuntu
- Wazuh Agent installation and registration on Windows host
- File Integrity Monitoring (FIM) — detective control operationalised
- Real-time dashboard monitoring via Kali Linux browser
- Compliance mapping to HIPAA and PCI-DSS within Wazuh
- Troubleshooting real deployment challenges (network issues, unreachable dashboard)

**Tools used:** `Wazuh` `Ubuntu` `Kali Linux` `Windows` `SIEM` `FIM`

## 🗂️ Project 4 — Small Business Risk Assessment: SecureBrew Coffee Shop

**File:** `SecureBrew-Risk-Assessment.pdf`

Risk assessment for SecureBrew — a small campus coffee shop undergoing digital transformation: a loyalty app (BeanStack), cloud-based POS system and public WiFi. Identified, categorised and treated risks introduced by each new technology.

**What was delivered:**
- Full risk register with Risk IDs, categories, likelihood and impact ratings
- Risk treatment options per identified risk
- Practical, actionable security recommendations for a non-technical business owner
- Applied risk communication — translating technical risk into plain language

**Why this matters:** Most organisations are small businesses. The ability to assess and communicate risk to non-technical stakeholders is one of the most valuable GRC skills.

## 🗂️ Project 5 — M365 Platform Project Risk Register

**File:** `M365-Project-Risk-Register.pdf`

A professional project risk register for the adoption and implementation of Microsoft M365 — produced to ICT Risk Manager standard (Version 1.0, ICT RM/2026). Covers risk identification, categorisation, impact assessment, and treatment across the full M365 deployment lifecycle.

**Structure:**
- Risk management framework overview
- Risk categories defined
- Risk management process
- Full project risk register with ownership, likelihood, impact and treatment

**This is a real-world deliverable format** — the kind of document that would sit in an actual project governance folder.
